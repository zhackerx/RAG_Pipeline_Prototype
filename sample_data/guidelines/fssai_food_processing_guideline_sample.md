# FSSAI Food Processing Guideline Sample (Prototype)

## Regulatory Baseline

1. Verify active FSSAI license and ensure category mapping aligns with product classes.
2. Confirm display of FSSAI license number on packaging and legal label panel.
3. Enforce product traceability from raw material lot to dispatch batch.
4. Ensure recall readiness procedures are documented and tested quarterly.

## Hygiene and Process Controls

1. Daily sanitation logs must be signed by QA supervisor.
2. Critical control points must include receiving, thermal processing, cooling, and packaging.
3. Any microbial deviation beyond control limits must trigger containment and batch hold.
4. Pest control observations must be closed within 48 hours.

## Documentation and Audit Signals

1. Internal audit closure pending beyond 30 days elevates risk.
2. Repeated minor non-conformities across 2 or more audits indicate process weakness.
3. CAPA evidence must include root cause, owner, due date, and closure proof.

## Loan Advisory Interpretation (Sample)

- High risk indicators:
  - Expired or mismatched FSSAI license
  - Recall plan absent or untested
  - Batch traceability below 80 percent
  - Repeated unresolved safety observations
- Medium risk indicators:
  - License renewal due within 30 days
  - Audit closure lag 15 to 30 days
  - Customer concentration above 40 percent
- Low risk indicators:
  - Strong CAPA closure discipline
  - Stable hygiene compliance trend
  - Complete traceability and labeling controls
